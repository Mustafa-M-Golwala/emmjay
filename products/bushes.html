Bushes

What is a Bush?

A bush (or bushing) is a mechanical lining designed to reduce friction and wear inside a hole, or to constrain, guide, or reduce the play in relative motion between two machine parts. Bushes serve as cost-effective and replaceable bearings and are commonly used in machinery, automotive components, and heavy equipment.

Applications

  • Automotive suspension systems
  • Gearboxes and motors
  • Pumps and compressors
  • Hydraulic and pneumatic cylinders
  • Industrial machinery and rotating equipment

Types of Bushes

  • Plain Bushes (Sleeve Bushes): Cylindrical sleeves for rotary shafts
  • Flanged Bushes: Bushes with a flange to prevent axial movement
  • Split Bushes: Slotted design for easy installation and flex
  • Sintered Bronze Bushes: Self-lubricating, ideal for low-load high-speed uses
  • Rubber Bushes: Absorb vibration, used in car suspensions and mounts
  • Polyurethane Bushes: Durable, wear-resistant, ideal for high-performance systems
  • Graphite Plugged Bushes: Lubrication-free for dry or harsh environments
  • Metal-Backed Composite Bushes: PTFE or polymer-lined bushes with steel backing

Advantages

  • Reduces wear between moving parts
  • Absorbs shocks and vibrations
  • Cost-effective replacement component
  • Wide range of sizes, types, and materials
  • Improves machinery lifespan and performance

Conclusion

Bushes are essential in reducing friction, guiding motion, and protecting machinery. With various types available for different applications, choosing the right bush enhances system efficiency and durability in mechanical designs.
