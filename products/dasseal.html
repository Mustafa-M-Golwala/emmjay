DAS Seals – Complete Guide

What is a DAS Seal?

A DAS seal is a double-acting composite piston seal designed to seal hydraulic cylinders that operate in both directions. DAS seals typically combine multiple components—an elastomeric sealing element, two thermoplastic support rings, and two guide rings—into a single integrated unit that provides sealing, guidance, and extrusion resistance.

Main Functions

  • Provide bidirectional sealing in hydraulic cylinders
  • Withstand high pressures and long stroke cycles
  • Prevent internal leakage between chambers
  • Offer guidance to the piston and reduce metal contact
  • Resist extrusion and deformation under load

Applications

  • Hydraulic cylinders in construction machinery
  • Mobile equipment (excavators, loaders, cranes)
  • Industrial presses and forging machines
  • Agricultural hydraulics
  • Heavy-duty piston applications

Structure of a DAS Seal

  • Central Sealing Element: Usually made from NBR or PU for elasticity and sealing
  • Backup Rings: Thermoplastic rings on either side to prevent extrusion
  • Guide Rings: Provide side load support and guide the piston in the cylinder

The compact, integrated construction simplifies installation and improves performance under extreme conditions.

Materials Used

  • NBR (Nitrile): General-purpose sealing element
  • Polyurethane (PU): Abrasion and wear resistance
  • PTFE or POM (Acetal): Guide rings and support elements
  • Fabric-Reinforced Rubber: For added strength in heavy-duty versions

Performance Characteristics

  • Pressure range: up to 400 bar (40 MPa), higher with reinforced versions
  • Temperature range: -30°C to +100°C (varies by material)
  • Speed: Suitable for standard piston speeds (0.5 to 1.0 m/s)
  • Resistant to hydraulic oils, greases, and some synthetic fluids

Advantages of DAS Seals

  • Pre-assembled, ready-to-install design
  • Excellent sealing in both directions (double-acting)
  • Integrated guidance and extrusion resistance
  • Compact and space-efficient construction
  • Long service life with minimal maintenance

Common Issues and Failures

  • Incorrect groove dimensions or tolerances
  • Improper installation damaging guide or backup rings
  • Chemical incompatibility with sealing element
  • Excessive side loading without adequate guidance

Conclusion

DAS seals offer a robust, efficient, and reliable solution for double-acting hydraulic pistons. With integrated support and guidance components, they are ideal for applications requiring high performance, durability, and ease of maintenance. Selecting the right material and configuration ensures long-term sealing success.
