Rubber Beading

Overview

Rubber beading is a flexible sealing component used to fill gaps, prevent vibration, and provide insulation. Available in various profiles and materials, it is essential in industries like automotive, construction, glass fitting, and HVAC.

Applications

  • Window and door sealing in buildings
  • Automotive door, trunk, and windshield sealing
  • Shock absorption in machinery and panels
  • Edge protection for metal sheets or panels
  • Dust and water sealing in electrical enclosures

Materials Used

  • EPDM: Excellent weather and UV resistance
  • Nitrile Rubber (NBR): Oil and fuel resistance
  • Silicone: High flexibility and heat resistance
  • Neoprene: Good flame and abrasion resistance

Types of Rubber Beading

  • Glazing Beading: For securing and sealing glass in doors and windows
  • Automotive Beading: Weatherproof and dustproof seals for vehicles
  • Edge Protector Beading: Clips onto sharp edges for safety and finish
  • U-Channel Beading: U-shaped profile used to hold panels or glass
  • D-Section Beading: D-profile for compressible sealing in doors
  • P-Section Beading: For weather-sealing with flexible compression
  • Co-Extruded Beading: Made with hard and soft rubbers for enhanced grip and flexibility
  • Sponge/Foam Beading: Compressible material for cushioning and thermal insulation

Advantages

  • Flexible and easy to install

Conclusion

Rubber beading is a versatile sealing and insulating solution used in a wide range of industries. With multiple types and material choices, it offers flexibility, protection, and performance for any sealing requirement.
